Hornet/Elf

The Elf and Hornet were longer, more up-market versions of the mini, they had a finned rear end and a larger boot. They featured luxury extras such as larger hubcaps, bumper over-riders, chrome accents and a wooden dashboard.

Traveller

The Traveller was an estate versions of the Mini. It had two van style doors rather than a boot. It had a wooden frame at the rear which earned it the nickname “Woodie”.

Van

The Mini Van was basically a commercial version of the Traveller in that the only difference between them was that the Van didn't have rear windows or a wooden frame. It proved to be very popular because it was less expensive than the car.

Moke

The Mini Moke was intended for use by the British Army. The original prototype had four-wheel-drive and was twin-engined, however it was never actually used by the military because of its poor ground clearance.

Pickup

The Mini Pickup was very similar to the Traveller and the Van, the only difference being that it had an open cargo area at the rear and a tailgate rather than doors.

Cooper

The original Mini Cooper had larger engine capacity increasing horsepower from 34 bhp to 55 bhp. It boasted upgrades such as a race tuned engine, twin SU carburettors disc brakes.
