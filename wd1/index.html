On a Miniature Mission

In 1957 post World War II England, the Suez Crisis meant petrol was rationed. People felt that the oversized gas guzzling cars didn't make sense anymore. Smaller cars such as the German bubble cars and the Italian Fiat 500 were beginning to be produced and sold successfully around Europe. Leonard Lord, head of British Motor Corporation (BMC) thought he could do one better than his German and Italian counterparts and set out on a mission to design a proper miniature car.

Alec's Angels

The task of fulfilling Lord's vision fell to BMC engineer Alec Issigonis. His remit; to build a fuel efficient car capable of transporting four adults which everyone could afford. In completing their task Issigonis and his team departed from traditional automobile design and a lot of their pioneering developments are still with us to this day. They mounted the engine transversely across the front axle and put the wheels right at the edge of the four corners of the car to maximize space in the cockpit.

Small and Mighty! But Why?

It turns out that Issigonis' design innovations, driven by necessity due to a fuel crisis had an unintended impact on its ability to match much more powerful cars in the motorsport world. Mounting the engine sideways at the front of the car improved grip and pushing the wheels to the four corners meant it handled like a go-kart, a fact that would be exploited to its fullest potential later on by John Cooper.

Potent Potential

John Cooper, a giant of British motorsport got his hands on the Mini in 1961, he fitted a larger engine, bigger brakes and with a few tweaks here and there the Mini Cooper was born. The Mini was transformed from a fuel efficient economy car to a rallying legend that would go on to win the prestigious Monte Carlo Rally three times in 1964, '65 and '67.

Car to the Stars!

The Mini was a car of the people and for the people. All the people. Celebrity owners of the humble Mini included Steve McQueen, Mick Jagger, Mr.Bean, and all of The Beatles. Paul McCartney's Mini was sold at auction for £182,000 in 2018. In 2017 Ringo Starr's Mini was bought at auction by Gerri Halliwell for £102,000. Ringo's car had been modified, converting it into a hatchback so that he could fit his drum-kit in the back.

The Legend Lives On?

BMW bought the Rover Group in 1994 and with it acquired the Mini. They brought out a redesigned version of the classic Mini in 2001 and are still producing new models to this day. The sports model of the BMW Minis still bears the Cooper name and competes in motorsport world wide just like their ancestors. Although BMW's re-imagined version of the Mini has been incredibly successful, many fans of the Classic Mini like myself, feel it lacks the soul of the original.
