The Name's Cooper, John Cooper

John Cooper was originally a toolmaker who served as an instrument maker in the Royal Air Force during World War II. He and his father, Charles owned a small garage in Surrey, England which specialized in maintaining race cars. In 1948 the pair set up the Cooper Car Company. They revolutionised Formula One by putting the engine behind the driver. This innovation helped them to win numerous Grand Prix.

Build It and They Will Race It!

John Cooper and Alec Issigonis were friends through competing against each other on the race track and also in a professional sphere because the Cooper Car Company bought engines from BMC. Cooper saw the racing potential of the Mini even before it had launched. Cooper eventually persuaded Issigonis to produce a GT version of the Mini having produced his own prototype and raced it at Monza, and so the Mini Cooper was born!

S For Small? No! S Is For SPEED!

The original Mini Cooper was such a success that an upgraded version was then produced. This version was called the Cooper S and boasted 70 bhp, a marked improvement on the original which had 55 bhp. This model was produced in two engine capacities 1275 cc and 970 cc specifically to race in the under 1000 cc and 1300 cc.

Big Things Come in Small Packages!

Three years after production, the Mini won its first Rally, the Tulip Rally in the Netherlands. This success would pave the way for an illustrious motorsports legacy which included wins at the Monte Carlo Rally three times in 1964, '65 and '67 and numerous successes in touring car championships and endurance races.
