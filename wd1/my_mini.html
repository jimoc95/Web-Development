Glory Days

My own Mini is a 1988 Mini City E which I bought when I was 16. I drove this car to my Leaving Cert and it brought me to my Debs and to and from college for about two months until the engine decided to blow up. The car was previously owned by a Nun who still had the original bill of sale and original users manual in the dashboard along with tons of religious paraphernalia.

Seen Better Days

Having begrudgingly sold the car to my Dad so I could afford to buy something more reliable, the car sat outside in the rain for a few years feeling sorry for itself and began rotting quite badly. It even had a dent on the rear as a result of my Dad pushing it around the yard with a tractor. When someone else came to buy the car, I decided I couldn't let it go so I bought it back off of my Dad. He charged me €50 more than he was charging the man who had come to buy the car.

They Don't Make 'Em Like They Used To

The car was so rotten underneath that it would need to be either fully restored or scrapped. The previous owner had tried to carry out some botched repairs on the underside with filler and even newspaper. The car was totally stripped back to bare metal to see the full extent of the rot and there was not much metal left.

YouTube University

In 2020 during lock-down I bought a mig welder and set about trying to learn how to weld from the University of YouTube. Eventually after plenty of terrible welding, I managed to put together a rotisserie jig so that the shell could be rotated to repair the floor panels underneath the car. The photo above is the current status of this project and as you can see there is a lot more to do.

Counting The Cost

It is very hard to get them in Ireland so I had to order the new panels needed from suppliers in the UK.
